I teach maths. But more than that, I study how people think about it — where they freeze, what clicks, and why the system keeps failing them. My work sits at the edge of cognitive science, education engineering, and mathematical thinking.

Right now I'm building **Tungston Theory** — a gamified intelligence system for maths learning. The goal isn't to make students faster at solving problems. It's to change how they see them.

I also build CV systems on the side because computer vision is just applied geometry and I can't help myself.
